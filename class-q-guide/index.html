The problem with Class Q

The rules changed in May 2024. Most guides haven't caught up.

Class Q permitted development rights allow you to convert an existing agricultural building into a residential dwelling without going through full planning permission. Up to ten dwellings per agricultural unit. Up to 1,000m² cumulative. No new build. No change of use application. Just prior approval.

But the rules are specific. The eligibility criteria are strict. And the things that kill applications — highways, bats, agricultural ties, structural assessments — are rarely explained clearly anywhere.

This guide covers every part of the process, in plain English, from someone who has been refused, challenged highways officers, gone to committee, and come out the other side with two approved applications on a working farm.
